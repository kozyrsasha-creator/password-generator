Why use a password generator?

A password generator helps you create strong, random passwords instantly. Human-made passwords often repeat patterns, include personal details, or use predictable sequences. A generator avoids these traps and produces strings that meet strict rules for security. You decide the length — from 8 to 64 characters — and whether to include uppercase, lowercase, digits, or symbols. Options such as no repeated characters, exclude ambiguous (O/0, l/1), or exclude brackets make it possible to adapt passwords to specific websites.

This free password generator works entirely in your browser. Nothing is stored or transmitted. That means you can generate a password while offline, confident that no third party has access. Need something short for an outdated form? Generate 12 characters. Creating a new account? Go with 16 or more. Protecting banking or work accounts? Choose 20+ characters. The longer the password, the stronger the protection.

What makes a strong password?

Strength comes from length and entropy. Six-letter words or common names are trivial to guess, while 16-character strings mixing different character sets create billions of possible combinations. Avoid dictionary words, birthdays, and phrases tied to your identity. If you prefer easier typing, a passphrase built from random words can be both practical and secure.

Best practices

Use a different password for every account. If one service is hacked, others remain protected. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever available. Combine this generator with a reputable password manager to save, organize, and autofill your credentials across devices. That way, you won’t need to remember dozens of random strings, yet each login stays unique.
